    Rules

    Fencing competitions are regulated by a set of rules that are designed to ensure the safety of the fencers, maintain fair play, and establish a standard for scoring.

    En Garde

    Fencers must start each bout in the en garde position, which involves standing with one foot in front of the other, knees slightly bent, with the weapon held in a ready position. Fencers must maintain this position throughout the bout and be ready to move and attack at any moment.

    Priority (Right of Way)

    In fencing, the fencer who initiates the attack first is awarded the "right of way" and has priority to score a point. However, the opponent has the opportunity to counterattack and take over the right of way. This rule encourages strategic timing and precision in attacks and defenses.

    Touches and Scoring

    Fencers score points by hitting their opponent with the tip or edge of their weapon. The target areas for scoring are different depending on the weapon used (foil, épée, or sabre). Touches are counted electronically, and the first fencer to reach a certain number of points (e.g., 15 points in foil and sabre) wins the bout.

    On-Guard Distance

    Fencers must maintain a safe distance from each other, known as on-guard distance, to avoid collisions and ensure fair play. The distance varies depending on the weapon used and the fencers' skill level, and it is important to constantly adjust and control this distance during the bout.

    Penalties

    Fencers can receive penalties for various infractions, such as stepping off the strip, covering target areas with their non-weapon hand, or showing unsportsmanlike behavior. Penalties can result in points being awarded to the opponent or even disqualification from the bout.

    Sportsmanship

    Fencers are expected to demonstrate good sportsmanship at all times, treating their opponents with respect and adhering to the rules of fair play. This includes shaking hands before and after the bout, accepting defeat graciously, and showing respect for the decisions of the referee.
